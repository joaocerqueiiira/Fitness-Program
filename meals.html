🍽️ Nutrition Guide

🥩 Why Protein Is Important

Protein is the most important nutrient for building and maintaining muscle. When you train, your muscles create tiny amounts of damage that need to be repaired. Protein provides the amino acids required for this repair process, helping your body recover stronger after each workout.

Protein is also highly filling, meaning it keeps you satisfied for longer compared to carbohydrates or fats. This can help reduce hunger and make a calorie deficit easier to maintain during a fat-loss phase.

Aim for approximately 1.6–2.2 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight each day. Good protein sources include chicken breast, lean beef, fish, eggs, Greek yogurt, protein yogurts, milk, and whey protein.

🍚 Why Carbohydrates Are Important

Carbohydrates are your body's primary source of energy. They provide fuel for workouts, daily activities, and recovery. Eating enough carbohydrates helps maintain training performance, allowing you to lift heavier weights and perform more repetitions over time.

Carbohydrates are stored inside the muscles as glycogen. During exercise, your body uses this stored glycogen for energy. If carbohydrate intake is too low, training performance can suffer and recovery may become slower.

Good carbohydrate sources include rice, potatoes, oats, fruit, wholegrain bread, wraps, pasta, and cereals.

🥑 Why Dietary Fat Is Important

Dietary fats play an essential role in overall health. They support hormone production, brain function, joint health, and the absorption of vitamins A, D, E, and K.

While fats are higher in calories than protein and carbohydrates, they are still important for long-term health and should not be completely removed from your diet.

Healthy fat sources include olive oil, avocados, nuts, nut butters, whole eggs, salmon, and other oily fish.

⏰ When Should You Eat?

Meal timing is far less important than total daily calorie and protein intake. However, many people find it beneficial to spread their meals evenly throughout the day.

A good approach is to eat a protein-rich meal every 3–5 hours. Consuming a meal containing protein and carbohydrates before training can help improve workout performance, while eating protein after training supports recovery.

The most important factor is consistency. Focus on hitting your daily calorie and protein targets first.

🔥 Understanding Calories

Calories are simply units of energy. If you consistently consume more calories than your body burns, you will gain weight. If you consistently consume fewer calories than your body burns, you will lose weight.

To lose body fat, most people should aim for a calorie deficit of approximately 300–500 calories below maintenance. This generally leads to sustainable fat loss while helping preserve muscle mass.

🧮 How To Estimate Your Calories

A simple starting point is:

  • Fat Loss: Body Weight (lbs) × 12
  • Maintenance: Body Weight (lbs) × 14–15
  • Muscle Gain: Body Weight (lbs) × 16–18

Example:

  • 180 lbs × 12 = 2160 calories for fat loss
  • 180 lbs × 15 = 2700 calories for maintenance
  • 180 lbs × 17 = 3060 calories for muscle gain

These are estimates. Monitor your body weight for 2–3 weeks and adjust calories based on your progress.

💧 Hydration Matters

Water is essential for performance, recovery, digestion, and overall health. Even mild dehydration can negatively impact strength, energy levels, and concentration.

Aim for at least 2–4 litres of water per day and increase intake if you train hard, sweat heavily, or live in a hot climate.

✅ Key Takeaway

Successful body transformations are built on four simple habits:

  • Hit your calorie target consistently.
  • Eat enough protein every day.
  • Train hard and progressively overload.
  • Stay patient and trust the process.

Small improvements repeated consistently lead to life-changing results.
