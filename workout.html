Why This Arnold Split Works

This Arnold Split is one of the most popular training programs because it allows each muscle group to be trained twice per week. Research shows that training muscles more than once per week can help increase muscle growth, strength, and overall fitness compared to only training them once. By hitting each muscle group every few days, you give your body enough stimulus to improve while still allowing adequate recovery between sessions.

The program is organised into Chest & Back, Arms & Shoulders, and Legs & Abs days. This structure allows you to focus on fewer muscle groups per workout, meaning you can train with more intensity and better technique. The combination of compound movements and isolation exercises helps build strength, improve muscle definition, and support fat loss when combined with a calorie-controlled diet.

Another benefit of this split is its balance between training volume and recovery. Each muscle group receives enough work to make progress without being overtrained. The inclusion of leg training, abdominal work, and daily step goals also helps increase calorie expenditure, making this an effective program for improving body composition and overall fitness.

Consistency is the key to success. Follow the workouts, hit your protein target, stay active throughout the day, and focus on gradual progress each week. Small improvements repeated over time lead to significant results.

📚 Form Tips & Technique Guide

Use these technique tips to maximise muscle growth, reduce injury risk, and get the most from every workout.

💪 Monday & Thursday – Chest & Back

  • Incline Smith Machine Press
    Keep your chest up and shoulder blades pulled back. Lower the bar to your upper chest with control and press upward without locking your elbows aggressively.
  • Machine Chest Press
    Keep your shoulders pinned against the pad. Press forward while squeezing your chest and slowly control the weight on the way back.
  • Pec Dec
    Keep a slight bend in your elbows and bring the handles together by squeezing your chest. Avoid using momentum.
  • Lat Pulldown
    Pull the bar toward your upper chest while driving your elbows down. Avoid leaning too far backward.
  • Machine Lat Row
    Keep your chest against the pad and pull through your elbows. Squeeze your lats at the end of every rep.
  • Upper Back Machine Row
    Pull toward your upper torso while keeping your chest tall. Focus on squeezing your shoulder blades together.

💪 Tuesday & Friday – Arms & Shoulders

  • Preacher Curl
    Keep your upper arms fixed on the pad. Curl the weight up under control and lower slowly for maximum tension.
  • Bayesian Curl
    Keep your elbow slightly behind your body throughout the movement. Focus on stretching the bicep at the bottom.
  • Forearm Curl
    Allow the weight to roll slightly into your fingers before curling your wrists upward.
  • Cable Reverse Curl
    Use an overhand grip and keep your elbows tucked. Focus on the forearms and brachialis.
  • Hammer Curl
    Keep palms facing each other and avoid swinging. Raise and lower the weight under control.
  • Tricep Pushdown
    Keep elbows pinned to your sides and fully extend your arms at the bottom while squeezing the triceps.
  • Overhead Tricep Extension
    Keep elbows pointing forward and stretch the triceps fully at the bottom of each rep.
  • Shoulder Press
    Press upward while keeping your core tight. Lower under control until your hands reach shoulder level.
  • Cable Lateral Raise
    Raise your arm out to the side until shoulder height. Lead with your elbow and avoid shrugging.

🦵 Wednesday & Saturday – Legs & Abs

  • Leg Press
    Keep your feet flat on the platform and lower until your knees reach roughly 90 degrees. Drive through your heels.
  • Stiff Leg Deadlift (SLDL)
    Push your hips back while keeping a slight bend in your knees. Feel a stretch in your hamstrings before returning upright.
  • Calf Raises (Straight Leg)
    Lower your heels fully for a stretch and rise onto your toes as high as possible while squeezing your calves.
  • Adductor Machine
    Keep your back against the pad and bring your legs together under control while squeezing your inner thighs.
  • Leg Extension
    Extend your legs fully and squeeze your quads at the top before lowering slowly.
  • Hamstring Curl
    Curl the weight toward your glutes while keeping your hips stable. Control the negative portion.
  • Cable Ab Crunch
    Crunch by bringing your ribs toward your hips rather than pulling with your arms.
  • Leg Raises
    Keep your lower back pressed down and raise your legs with control. Avoid swinging.

Perfect form beats heavier weight every time. Master the movement first, then progressively overload.
